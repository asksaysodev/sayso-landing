## Why Agents Search for Shilo AI Alternatives

Shilo AI is not a bad product. It's actually quite impressive. It does what it says — listens to your agents' calls, grades them, provides transcripts, and gives feedback your team leads can use in coaching sessions. For teams that had zero visibility into call quality before, that's genuinely valuable.

But here's the pattern we see over and over: a team lead invests in Shilo, starts reviewing call grades, and realizes the same problems keep showing up. Agents freeze when a seller says "we're not interested." Agents pitch too early when a buyer says "we're just looking." The grades confirm what everyone already suspected — but they don't fix the behavior in real time.

That's the gap, and why people start searching for Shilo alternatives, shilo.ai alternatives, or the best Shilo alternative for their team. They don't need more proof that calls are going sideways. They need something that intervenes before the call goes sideways.

## Why Teams Move Away From Shilo AI

### Post-Call Feedback Arrives Too Late

Shilo grades calls after they happen. By the time an agent sees their score, the prospect has moved on. The missed appointment is already missed. Post-call analytics are useful for spotting trends over weeks and months, but they can't help an agent recover a conversation that's falling apart right now.

### Cost Adds Up Quickly for Mid-Size Teams

According to publicly available competitor comparisons, mid-size teams averaging around 90 hours of graded calls can expect to pay roughly $1,500 - $1,800/month for Shilo's AI capabilities alone. That's a significant line item — especially when it doesn't directly change outcomes.
### Role Play Doesn't Translate to Live Calls

Shilo added AI role-play scenarios , which is a helpful addition for training. But practice and performance are two different things. An agent can crush a role-play with a simulated expired listing seller and still freeze when a real one pushes back with "my neighbor's agent said they could get more." The gap between practice and live execution is exactly where most agents lose appointments.

### It Doesn't Coach — It Reports

There's an important difference between a tool that tells you what went wrong and a tool that helps you get it right in the moment. Shilo is a reporting and analysis layer. It's excellent at identifying patterns — which agents ask for the appointment, which ones talk too much, which ones miss objections. But it doesn't show the right question in your ear when the prospect says something unexpected.

## What to Look For in a Shilo AI Replacement

### Real-Time Guidance During Live Calls

The single most impactful upgrade from post-call grading is in-call coaching. Look for tools that listen to the live conversation and surface prompts — the next question to ask, the right way to handle an objection, a real-time reminder to ask for the appointment. This is what actually changes agent behavior on the spot.

### Built Specifically for Real Estate Outbound

General sales coaching tools exist, but real estate conversations have unique dynamics. Expired listing objections are different from SaaS objections. FSBO sellers have specific concerns that a generic AI won't understand. Make sure whatever tool you choose was trained on real estate call patterns, not B2B enterprise sales.

### Works With Your Existing Tech Stack

Your team already has a CRM, a dialer, maybe a lead gen platform. A coaching tool should layer on top of what you already use — not force you to rip and replace. The best alternatives integrate with Follow Up Boss, BoldTrail, Sierra Interactive, and other RE-specific platforms.

### Transparent and Predictable Pricing

Hidden costs kill budgets. Look for pricing that's straightforward — either per-agent or per-team — without surprise overages based on call volume or minutes graded.

## What This Actually Looks Like on a Real Call

Here's a scenario every team lead has witnessed:

### The Common Version (No Live Coaching)

**Agent:** "Hi, this is Sarah with Realty Group. I saw your listing expired last week — are you still looking to sell?"

**Prospect:** "Yeah, but we're taking a break. The whole experience was frustrating."

**Agent:** "I totally understand. Well, if you change your mind, we'd love to help. We have a great marketing plan and—"

**Prospect:** "Okay, thanks." *Click.*

The agent made a classic mistake: the prospect opened the door with the word "frustrating," and instead of asking what was frustrating, the agent jumped into a pitch. Shilo's post-call grade would flag this correctly — "missed opportunity to ask a discovery question." But by then, the prospect is gone.

### The Better Version (With Real-Time Coaching)

**Agent:** "Hi, this is Sarah with Realty Group. I saw your listing expired last week — are you still looking to sell?"

**Prospect:** "Yeah, but we're taking a break. The whole experience was frustrating."

*[Agent sees a real-time prompt: "Ask what made it frustrating. Let them talk."]*

**Agent:** "That makes sense. What was the most frustrating part for you?"

**Prospect:** "Honestly? We had maybe three showings in two months. I don't think our agent did much marketing beyond sticking it on the MLS."

*[Agent sees a prompt: "Validate, then ask about their timeline."]*

**Agent:** "Three showings in two months — that would frustrate me too. If the right plan were in place, would you still want to sell this year?"

**Prospect:** "I mean, yeah, if someone could actually get buyers through the door."

The conversation stays alive. The agent asks better questions. The agent has a real shot at booking an appointment . That's the difference between knowing what to do and doing it when it counts.

## Non-Software Alternatives Worth Considering

Software isn't the only path to better calls. Here are a few approaches teams use, each with tradeoffs:

**Hiring a dedicated sales trainer or coach:** Effective but expensive ($500-$2,000+/month). The coach can only work with a few agents at a time, and the advice doesn't always translate to what happens mid-call.

** Role-play sessions:** Free and genuinely helpful when done consistently. The problem is consistency — life gets in the way, and the quality depends on who's running the session.

**Manager ride-alongs (listen-in coaching):** Arguably the most effective coaching method. A manager listens to a live call, slides notes during call, and debriefs immediately after. The downside: it doesn't scale. One manager can't ride along with 15 agents.

**Script libraries and call playbooks:** Useful for new agents building a foundation. Limited for experienced agents who already know the scripts but freeze when the conversation goes off-script.
