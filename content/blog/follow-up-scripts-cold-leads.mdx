Knowing how to follow up with cold leads is where most agents quietly lose half their pipeline. A lead goes quiet for a few months, you assume they lost interest, and the file goes cold for good. The truth is most cold leads did not lose interest, they forgot you exist. Below are three openers that get replies, a re-engagement cadence that does not feel desperate, and the exact words to use the moment they write back.

This is one piece of the larger system in our guide to [real estate follow up scripts](/blog/real-estate-follow-up-scripts/), but cold leads behave differently than fresh ones and need their own playbook.

## Why Cold Leads Go Cold (and What It Changes About Your Follow-Up)

Most leads do not go cold because they decided not to move. They go cold because life got busy and you stopped being the agent they remember. Roughly 40% of internet leads buy or sell within 24 months of filling out a form, and most of them transact with a different agent, the one who kept showing up after everyone else went silent.

Before you write a single message, figure out why this lead went quiet. The reason changes the opener.

- **You dropped them.** You made two attempts, heard nothing, and moved on. The lead is not avoiding you, they barely registered you. Your opener needs to re-establish who you are without an apology.
- **They stalled on purpose.** They told you "maybe next year" or "we want to see what happens with rates," and the timeline was always far out. This lead is not cold, they are long-term. Pushing for a call now gets you muted.
- **They got busy and forgot.** They were engaged, then a job change, a kid's schedule, or a failed offer pulled them away. A specific, recent reason to re-engage brings them right back.

Here is the principle underneath all three: you are not a listing portal. Agents who only forward links and blast "just checking in" texts are interchangeable, so they get ignored. The agent who re-enters the conversation with a specific insight the lead cannot find on their own looks like a strategist worth replying to.

<CalloutBox type="key-takeaway">
Cold leads are not cold because they lost interest. They are cold because you stopped being memorable. Lead with something useful and specific and you become the agent they call when they are finally ready.
</CalloutBox>

## 3 Cold Lead Follow-Up Scripts That Actually Get Replies

The opener that revives a cold lead does one job: it gives them a reason to reply that has nothing to do with you needing the business. Lead with something specific and recent, then give them an easy, low-stakes way to respond. Generic outreach gets single-digit reply rates. Specific pattern interrupts pull 20% or more.

**Script 1: The neighbor-sale pattern interrupt.** Works for buyers and sellers because a nearby sale is relevant to anyone with a stake in the area. The curiosity hook is the "story," not the price.

<ScriptExample label="Neighbor-Sale Pattern Interrupt (Text)">
Hey [Name], not sure if a move is still on your radar, but a home two streets over from [Street] just sold for [amount] in 9 days. It made me think of you. Want the quick story on why it moved that fast?
</ScriptExample>

**Script 2: The graceful exit.** This is the highest-reply opener for a lead you personally dropped. Counterintuitively, giving the lead permission to say no makes them far more likely to say yes. When you remove the pressure to reply, you also remove the reason to ignore you, and the honesty reads as confidence rather than desperation.

<ScriptExample label="Graceful Exit (Text or Voicemail)">
Hi [Name], it's [Your Name]. We talked back in [Month] about [buying/selling] in [Area], and then life got busy on both ends. Totally fine if your plans changed, just tell me either way so I am not chasing a ghost. Still thinking about a move, or did that shift?
</ScriptExample>

**Script 3: The criteria reset.** Use this for a buyer lead who has been silently receiving your automated home alerts. Instead of asking if they are still interested, you ask the one question that re-qualifies them and makes your next message useful.

<ScriptExample label="Criteria Reset (Text)">
Hey [Name], you have been getting my [Area] home alerts for a while and I would rather send you 3 great ones than 30 random ones. What is the one feature a home would need for you to actually want to walk through it? I will rebuild your list around that.
</ScriptExample>

<CalloutBox type="warning">
Never open a cold-lead message with "just checking in" or "following up to see if you're still interested." Both signal you have nothing new to say, and a yes-or-no question like "are you still interested?" is the easiest thing in the world to ignore. Lead with a fact: a sale on their street, a rate move, a listing that fits their old criteria.
</CalloutBox>

## What to Say After a Cold Lead Replies

Getting a reply is not the win, it is the start. The moment a cold lead writes back, your job shifts from re-engagement to qualification: extract a timeline and a reason, then offer a specific time to talk. Most agents blow this by staying in "helpful nurture" mode, sending more listings, more market updates, never asking for the conversation.

When a revived lead gives you a soft answer like "just keeping an eye on things," do not retreat. Validate it, then ask one question that pins down the timeline so your next move is informed.

<ScriptExample label="When a Cold Lead Says 'Just Keeping an Eye on Things'">
Makes sense, a lot of people watch for a while before something clicks. Quick question so I send you the right things and not noise: are you closer to "ready if the perfect one shows up" or "probably next year"? Either answer is fine, it just changes how often you hear from me.
</ScriptExample>

That single question sorts the lead and earns you the right to ask for time. Once they signal any real intent, transition to a specific, short, two-option ask instead of an open-ended "let me know."

<ScriptExample label="The Appointment Transition">
Based on what you just said about [detail from their reply], a quick 15-minute call will save you weeks of guessing. I have 4:15 tomorrow or 10:30 Friday. Which one works?
</ScriptExample>

The transition references something they actually said, keeps the call short to lower resistance, and forces a decision on timing rather than on whether to meet at all. ## How Often to Follow Up with a Cold Lead

Once a lead is genuinely cold, 12 or more months from any move, contact them once a quarter with something useful, not once a week with a check-in. Over-contacting a long-timeline lead gets you muted. Under-contacting loses them to whoever shows up at month 13.

There are two distinct cadences, and mixing them up is what kills re-engagement:

- **The revival burst.** When you first try to wake a cold lead, you get one or two specific touches across text and call. If they engage, you move into a real conversation and a normal follow-up rhythm. If they go silent again, you drop them to quarterly.
- **The long-term quarterly touch.** For leads who are real but far out, send one genuinely valuable touch per quarter: a major rate shift, a new development announcement, a quarterly snapshot of their target area. The goal is authority and consistency, not asking for anything.

The numbers back the patience. 80% of sales require at least 5 follow-ups, yet most agents quit after 2. One well-timed touch per quarter is enough to stay the agent they remember without becoming the agent they mute. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should you say to a cold real estate lead?

Open with a specific, recent fact rather than a check-in: a home that sold on their street, a rate change, or a listing that matches their old criteria. Give them a graceful exit by acknowledging their plans may have changed, then ask one short question that invites a quick reply. Avoid "are you still interested?" because it is too easy to ignore.

### Do cold real estate leads ever convert?

Yes. Roughly 40% of internet leads buy or sell within 24 months of the original form fill, and most of them transact with the agent who kept following up after everyone else stopped. A cold lead is rarely a dead lead, it is usually a lead with a longer timeline than you expected.

### How often should you follow up with a cold lead?

For genuinely cold leads who are 12+ months out, once per quarter with something valuable is the right cadence. Contact them more than that without a specific reason and you get muted. When a cold lead re-engages, switch to a normal follow-up rhythm built around the next scheduled step.

### Should you call or text a cold lead first?

Text usually wins for the first re-engagement touch because it is low-pressure and gets a 98% open rate, but pairing a short voicemail with a text in the same window lifts reply rates. Once the lead responds, move to a call to build the relationship that actually leads to a booked appointment.
