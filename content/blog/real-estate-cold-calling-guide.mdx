Cold calling in real estate is not dead — it is just done badly by most agents. The agents who book appointments consistently do not have some secret talent. They have better scripts, better timing, and better responses when prospects push back.

This guide gives you everything you need: proven cold call scripts for every scenario, frameworks for handling the most common objections, and a system for turning uncomfortable phone conversations into booked appointments.

## Expired Listing Scripts

Expired listings are one of the highest-converting cold call sources in real estate. The homeowner already wanted to sell — they just had a bad experience. Your job is to acknowledge that frustration and position yourself as the solution.

**The opening script:**

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Brokerage]. I noticed your home on [Street] came off the market recently, and I wanted to reach out. I know that can be frustrating — especially when you were ready to move. Would it be okay if I asked what happened?"

This script works because it does not pitch. It asks permission to have a conversation. Most expired listing homeowners want to vent about their experience, and that gives you an opening to listen, empathize, and eventually offer a different approach.

## FSBO Scripts

For Sale By Owner homeowners are not anti-agent — they are anti-paying for something they think they can do themselves. The key is to offer value without being condescending.

> "Hi [Name], I saw your home listed for sale on [Platform]. I actually work with buyers in [Area] and I wanted to ask — are you open to working with agents who bring you a qualified buyer, or are you handling everything yourself?"

This positions you as someone with buyers, not someone trying to take their listing. It lowers resistance immediately.

## Circle Prospecting Scripts

[Circle prospecting](/glossary/circle-prospecting) is calling homeowners near a recent sale or listing. It is one of the easiest cold call scripts because you have a natural reason to call.

> "Hi [Name], I wanted to let you know — a home just sold on your street at [Address] for [Price]. I thought you might be curious about what that means for your home's value. Is that something you would be interested in hearing about?"

The key is specificity. Mention the exact address, the sale price, and the neighborhood. Generic calls get rejected. Specific calls get conversations.

## How to Start a Real Estate Call

The first 10 seconds of any cold call determine whether the prospect stays on the line or hangs up. Most agents fail here because they launch into a pitch instead of earning permission to talk.

**The pattern interrupt:**

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. I know I am catching you out of the blue — is this a bad time for a 30-second call?"

This works because it is honest and respectful. It acknowledges the interruption, which makes the prospect more likely to give you time.

## Buyer Lead Scripts

Inbound buyer leads require a different approach than cold outreach. These prospects raised their hand — your job is to qualify quickly and set the appointment before they talk to three other agents.

> "Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out about [Property/Area]. I wanted to connect quickly — are you actively looking right now, or just starting to explore the idea?"

The qualifying question separates tire-kickers from motivated buyers in one sentence.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Scripts are starting points, not crutches.** Use them to build confidence, then adapt them to your natural voice.
2. **The first 10 seconds matter most.** Earn permission to talk before you pitch anything.
3. **Objections are not rejections.** "Not ready yet" usually means "give me a reason to keep talking."
4. **Consistency beats talent.** Agents who make 30 calls a day with decent scripts outperform agents who make 5 calls with perfect scripts.
5. **Log every call.** The deal you close in 6 months comes from the note you wrote today.

## FAQ

**What is the best cold calling script for real estate?**
There is no single best script — the best script matches the lead source (expired, FSBO, circle prospecting, buyer lead) and sounds natural coming from you. Start with the frameworks above and adapt them to your voice.

**How many cold calls should a real estate agent make per day?**
Most productive agents make 25–50 calls per day. Consistency matters more than volume — 30 focused calls beats 100 rushed ones.

**What time is best for real estate cold calling?**
Tuesday through Thursday, 10am–12pm and 4pm–6pm tend to get the highest contact rates. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons.

**How do you handle rejection on cold calls?**
Reframe rejection as information. "Not interested" is not personal — it means this prospect is not ready right now. Log the conversation, set a follow-up task, and move to the next call. The numbers always work in your favor over time.
