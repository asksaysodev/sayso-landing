# **MaverickRE Alternatives: 7 Better Options for Real Estate Teams**

## **TLDR**

* If you’re searching **MaverickRE alternatives** because coaching shows up after the call, look at tools that help during the call, not just after it.  
* If your real issue is accountability and visibility across agents, you want an operating system style tool, not another dialer.  
* If you need speed to lead, routing, and clean contact work, you may be better off tightening your CRM and calling workflow instead of adding another layer.  
* The “best” replacement depends on what you expected MaverickRE to fix: manager oversight, agent behavior on calls, or daily execution.

## **Introduction**

If you run a team, manage ISAs, or you’re the broker trying to keep standards consistent, you already know the ugly truth: call volume is not the problem. The calls are happening. The conversations are just not being led well enough to reliably book appointments.  
MaverickRE sits in the “sales performance and coaching” category. It’s built around analytics, AI coaching, and call grading layered on top of your existing systems. ([MaverickRE](https://www.maverickre.com/blog/real-estate-sales-management-software))  
So when someone asks for alternatives, it’s usually not because MaverickRE is “bad.” It’s because the team needs a different kind of help than what a post call platform can realistically deliver at scale.

## **Why teams look for MaverickRE alternatives**

To be clear on what you’re replacing, MaverickRE is positioned as analytics plus AI driven coaching and call grading, pulling data from other systems rather than being your CRM. (MaverickRE)  
That works great when the main problem is leadership visibility and accountability. It breaks down when the problem is execution on the live call.  
Here’s what I see most often when a team starts shopping MaverickRE alternatives.

### **1\) The coaching happens after the call, and the moment is gone**

Most teams do some version of this:

* Agent takes 30 to 60 inbound leads a week  
* Leader reviews call scores or call snippets later  
* Coaching happens days later in a one on one, or not at all  
* Agent goes right back into the same patterns on the next call

That delay is the whole issue. The call is over. The prospect moved on. The agent barely remembers what they said, and they definitely do not remember what they were thinking when they froze.  
Real coaching is timing. If the correction comes after the moment, it becomes theory.

### **2\) You end up managing the platform instead of managing behavior**

Dashboards can become a trap.  
If your leaders are spending their week:

* checking scores  
* chasing adoption  
* building scorecards  
* trying to interpret trends

…you are still not changing the daily call outcomes. You are just moving your time from live coaching to system administration.

### **3\) Teams confuse “activity tracking” with “conversation control”**

A lot of platforms help you measure activity. Fewer platforms help you control what happens when a prospect says:

* “We’re just looking.”  
* “We already have an agent.”  
* “We might sell later this year.”  
* “We’re not ready.”

The conversion breakdown is usually right there. The agent takes the comment at face value, then starts rambling, then loses the appointment.

### **4\) You may not need another layer at all**

Sometimes the simplest answer is:

* your CRM is messy  
* your routing is loose  
* your call workflow is inconsistent  
* your standards are unclear

In that case, the best alternative is the tool that makes your execution simple and repeatable, even if it is less “advanced.”

## **What to get clear on before you switch**

Before you pick a replacement, answer one question honestly:  
**What did you want MaverickRE to change in your business?**  
Most teams fall into one of these buckets.

### **Bucket A: Leadership visibility and accountability**

You want:

* clean reporting across lead sources  
* agent activity standards  
* performance coaching based on real data

### **Bucket B: Better phone conversion right now**

You want:

* better questions  
* tighter control of objections  
* more booked appointments  
* fewer blown conversations

### **Bucket C: A cleaner core stack**

You want:

* one place to work leads  
* fewer tools  
* consistent routing, calling, and tracking

Once you know your bucket, use these criteria to evaluate alternatives.

### **Practical criteria I would use as an operator**

**1\) Where does the tool help, during or after the call?**  
If you need better calls, “after” is often too late.  
**2\) How much manager time does it require each week?**  
A tool that needs constant policing will get dropped.  
**3\) Does it fit how your team actually works leads?**  
If your workflow is PPC, sign calls, open house leads, and database reactivation, the tool needs to support that rhythm.  
**4\) Does it tighten execution for new agents?**  
If a new agent needs 90 days before they sound decent on the phone, your ramp cost is too high.  
**5\) Can it plug into your existing systems without drama?**  
Most teams are not switching CRMs just to test a platform.

## **How to choose the right alternative based on what is breaking on calls**

Here’s the operator test I use.

### **If you lose appointments because agents do not know what to say next**

Pick a tool that helps during the call.  
This is the classic situation:

* Prospect says “We might sell later.”  
* Agent says “Okay, no problem, just let me know.”  
* Call ends politely.  
* Nothing is scheduled.

That agent did not need more motivation. They needed a better next question, right then.

### **If you lose appointments because the team is inconsistent and leaders lack visibility**

Pick an accountability and operating system style tool.  
This is the situation where:

* standards are unclear  
* daily activity is uneven  
* leaders cannot see performance quickly enough to correct it

Tools like Sisu fit this better than a dialer, and better than a pure coaching overlay.

### **If you lose appointments because execution is sloppy**

Tighten the core workflow first.  
If your speed to lead is slow, your routing is messy, or agents are working leads in five different places, a coaching tool will not fix the operational leak.  
One missed listing appointment can be a five figure mistake in a lot of markets. You do not need perfect analytics to avoid that. You need clean execution.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

## **Is MaverickRE a CRM?**

No, it is generally positioned as a layer that connects to your existing systems rather than replacing your CRM. (MaverickRE)

## **What is the biggest reason teams switch away from MaverickRE?**

Most teams switch because they want a different kind of leverage, either real time help on calls or a simpler core system. They are usually trying to close the timing gap between the call and the coaching.

## **Do I need call grading or real time call guidance?**

If your issue is leadership visibility and accountability, call grading can help. If your issue is agents losing control on live calls, real time guidance tends to move the needle faster.

## **Can I keep my CRM and still improve call conversion?**

Yes, many teams keep their CRM and add a dialer or a coaching layer depending on what is missing. The key is making sure you are solving the actual breakdown, not adding tools.

## **Are all in one platforms better for teams?**

They can be, if your team wants consolidation and standardization. They can also be too heavy if you already have lead sources and only need a specific function.
